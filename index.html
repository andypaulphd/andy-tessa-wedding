Getting to Asheville

Asheville has its own regional airport, but often it makes more sense to fly into Charlotte (CLT), which is a two hour drive away. Even though you'll be renting a car for a couple of days, you'll likely save some money with cheaper airfare. And you 'll probably save time too, since flying into Asheville means you'll need to make a connection in somewhere like Atlanta or Charlotte anyways.

What to Do in Asheville

Here's a short list of things to do while you're in town, starting with quick diversions and ending with longer excursions.

The Grove Arcade

1 Page Ave, Asheville, NC 28801

Downtown Asheville is small and easily walkable, so you should feel free to explore if you have an hour or two. But this is a good place to start and it's Gerry's favorite. It's a historic building that was renovated with shops, restaurants, and our favorite wine bar, Santé. (Also check out the nearby bookstore Malaprop's at 55 Haywood St., one of the biggest indie bookstores in the Southeast.)

Botanical Gardens

151 W T. Weaver Boulevard, Asheville, NC 28804

The Botanical Gardens are right next to the University of North Carolina Asheville, which are both just a mile from downtown—a short car ride and free parking and admission.

Asheville Brewery District ("The South Slope")

Asheville has more beer breweries per capita than anywhere else, at least last time we checked. You can walk to the South Slope from anywhere downtown, and visit a good number of them. (Check out Bhramari Brewing Company or Catawba Brewing Company for starters.)

Biltmore Estate

Link to the Biltmore Website

This is the place to go if you have a full day in Asheville. It's a really big house. They have tours of the house and grounds, and they also have their own winery where you can go for tastings.
